RAJGAD FORT

Rajgad Fort

Rajgad Fort, perched at about 1,376 m in the Sahyadri range roughly 60 km southwest of Pune, stands as the original capital of Shivaji’s Maratha Empire for over 26 years after being captured in 1647 and renamed from Murumbdev – hence the nickname “King of Fort”.The sprawling fort spans around 40 km in base diameter, featuring multi-tiered defenses, rock-hewn bastions, cisterns, caves, temples, and palace ruins, all offering rich insight into 17th‑century military architecture.

Visitors can explore four main zones: the expansive Padmavati Machi with its temple, freshwater lake, and Saibai’s Samadhi; the towering three-layered Sanjeevani Machi; the narrow Suvela Machi with its dramatic ridges; and the Balekilla citadel — the fort’s summit offering sweeping vistas of adjacent peaks including Torna, Sinhagad, and Raigad.

Adventurers can choose from three main trekking routes beginning at Gunjavane (via the dramatic Chor Darwaza, about 2.5–3 hr, moderate to difficult), Pali (easier, ~2 hr), or the more rugged Bhutonde/Bhor path (~3–4 hr). The trek is especially scenic post-monsoon (October–February) and during winter.

There’s no entry fee, and facilities at Padmavati Temple support overnight stays of around 50 people; local food options include poha, pithla-bhakri, and tea, though carrying water, snacks, sturdy shoes, rain gear, and headlamps is recommended.
