FORTS

RAJGAD FORT

Rajgad Fort, perched at 1,376 m in the Sahyadri range, was the first capital of the Maratha Empire under Shivaji Maharaj. It remained the capital for over 25 years. The fort has a rich history and unique architecture with palaces, caves, water cisterns, and three main machis (fortified plateaus). Rajgad was previously known as Murumbdev. The fort is a favorite trekking destination today.

TORNA FORT

Torna Fort, also called Prachandagad, stands tall at 1,403 m and is the highest hill fort near Pune. This fort was the first to be captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj at the age of 16 in 1646, marking the beginning of the Maratha Empire. It offers breathtaking views and is known for its massive size and strategic location. The fort has temples, bastions, and unique rock formations.

SHIVNERI FORT

Shivneri Fort, near Junnar in Pune district, is the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (born on 19 February 1630). Built in the 6th century by the Yadavas, the fort has witnessed many historical events. Inside, you can find water tanks, temples, and statues of Shivaji Maharaj and Jijamata. Its location on a steep hill made it a stronghold in medieval times.

RAIGAD FORT

Raigad Fort, situated at 1,356 m near Mahad, served as the capital of the Maratha Empire. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was crowned here in 1674. The fort has massive gates, secret pathways, and remains of palaces. It offers panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountains and has a ropeway for visitors today. The fort holds immense historical significance for Marathas.

MURUD-JANJIRA FORT

Murud-Janjira is a unique sea fort located on an island off the Raigad coast. It is famous for being unconquered despite numerous attacks. Built in the 17th century by the Siddis, it has 19 bastions, freshwater ponds, and secret passages. The fort is accessible only by boats and showcases a blend of Mughal and Indian architecture. It remains a symbol of naval strength.

SINHAGAD FORT

Sinhagad Fort, located near Pune, is a historic fort known for the legendary battle of Tanaji Malusare in 1670. Originally called Kondhana, it was renamed Sinhagad (Lion’s Fort) after the Maratha victory. The fort has steep slopes, bastions, and monuments dedicated to Tanaji. Today, it is a popular trekking and picnic spot offering scenic views of the Sahyadri hills.
