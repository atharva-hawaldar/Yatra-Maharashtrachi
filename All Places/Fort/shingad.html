SHINHGAD FORT

, perched atop a rugged triangular hill near Junnar, serves as the revered birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The well-maintained trek begins in Junnar and leads through seven historical gates, with a thrilling chain-clad path adding daring flair for adventure lovers. Within the fort lies the Shivkunj pavilion—where Shivaji's cradle once rested—alongside the serene Shivai Devi Temple and Jijabai’s ancestral chamber. Water is wisely preserved in central Badami pond and twin Ganga–Jamuna springs, while the granary (Ambarkhana) and mosque reflect the fort’s multifaceted legacy. At Kadelot Point, sheer cliffs offer a chilling reminder of medieval discipline and breathtaking vistas. Trekking here is feasible year-round, but winter offers clear skies for exploring and monsoon dress the hills in emerald green. With basic facilities at the base and rich historical layers on top, Shivneri presents an accessible one-day pilgrimage for history aficionados, trekkers, and cultural explorers alike.

Quick Facts

Location:
2–3 km from Junnar in Pune district, ~90 km from Pune, ~150 km from Mumbai
Nearest City:
Junnar – well-connected by road and public transport
Trek Duration:
Seven‑Gates route: ~2–3 km, ~1–1.5 hr ascent, plus 1–2 hr to explore. Chain‑Gate route: steeper, ~45 min
Height:
~1,024 m (3,360 ft) above sea level
