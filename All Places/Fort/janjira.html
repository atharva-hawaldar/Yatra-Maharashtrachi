Murud‑Janjira Fort

Murud‑Janjira Fort

is a majestic sea fortress perched on an oval rock just off the shore of Murud, rising above the Arabian Sea with its imposing black‑granite ramparts and 26 artillery towers. Initially a humble wooden structure built by fishermen in the 15th century, it was fortified into a stone stronghold by Malik Ambar in 1567, later becoming an unbeatable bastion under the ruling Siddis. Notable for its massive cannons—Kalal Baangadi, Chavri, and Landa Kasam—the fort’s defences were never breached by the Marathas, Mughals, Portuguese, or British. Entry is via a narrow main gate facing Rajpuri, with a secret sea gate (“Darya Darwaza”) for emergency escape. Inside are remnants of a royal palace, mosque, officers’ quarters, stables, granaries, and impressive freshwater ponds over 60 feet deep—remarkable for an island fort. Visitors board a boat from Rajpuri jetty (operational between 7 AM–12 PM and 2–4:45 PM), with no entry fee and boat fares around ₹20–500 depending on boat size. The fort is open 7 AM–6 PM, making October to March the ideal season for a calm, enriching visit. After exploring the fort, travelers can enjoy nearby Murud Beach, marvel at Garambi Falls and Ahmedganj Palace, trek to Kasa Fort, visit the lush Phansad Sanctuary, or explore ancient Kuda Caves and vibrant Kashid Beach. With its dramatic sea setting, resilient architecture, and serene surroundings, Murud‑Janjira offers a captivating blend of history, adventure, and coastal beauty.

Quick Facts

Location:
Island sea-fort built on an oval rock off the coast of Murud town, Raigad district, Maharashtra
Defense & Architecture:
Massive 40-foot black granite walls with 26 artillery towers and originally over 500 cannons; key guns include Kalal Baangadi, Chavri, and Landa Kasam, famed for their long range

Activities

Make the most of your visit to this heritage destination by ticking these activities off your list.

  • Take a boat ride to the fort from Rajpuri Jetty
  • Enter through the main sea-facing gate (Maha Darwaja)
  • Explore the interior ruins of the palace, mosque, and officers’ quarters
  • Walk along the massive stone walls and bastions
  • See the famous giant cannons, including Kalal Bangdi
  • Visit the two freshwater ponds inside the fort
  • Enjoy panoramic sea views from the top of the fort
