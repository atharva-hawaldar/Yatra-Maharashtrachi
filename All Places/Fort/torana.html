TORANA FORT

Torna Fort, also known historically as Prachandagad, soars to 1,403 m above sea level, making it Pune district’s highest hill-fort. Situated roughly 60 km from Pune, the trek begins from the base village of Velhe, you can spot the majestic fort from the start. The ascent covers 2–3 km of mixed terrain, including a steep ridge where ladders and railings guide trekkers through rocky patches. On the way up, monsoon-season visitors are treated to vibrant wildflowers and occasional streams. It typically takes about three hours to reach the summit, where explorers can spend another 3–4 hours wandering through historical relics such as Budhla Machi, Zunjar Machi, Bini and Kothi Darwajas, and the Menghai Devi Temple nestled amidst the ruins. The panorama from the top encompasses numerous other forts, including Rajgad and Raigad. Trekkers should set out between July and September to experience lush greenery, or from November to February for crisp skies and cooler temperatures. Logistics are straightforward: Velhe offers basic food stalls, though there is no potable water on the fort—carry at least three liters. Emergency support is available via local health and rescue teams, but mobile signal is patchy beyond the base. Overall, Torna Fort offers a fulfilling blend of physical challenge, rich history, and breathtaking natural beauty—ideal for seasoned adventurers and fit beginners alike.

Quick Facts

Location:
Approximately 60–65 km southwest of Pune; base village is Velhe
Nearest City:
Velhe – trek starts beside local police station
Trek Duration:
Gunjavane route: 3 hrs ascent; additional 3–4 hrs on top to explore
Height:
1,403 m (4,603 ft) – the tallest hill-fort in Pune district

Activities

Make the most of your visit to this heritage destination by ticking these activities off your list.

  • Trek to the fort via Velhe village, the most popular base
  • Enter through the main gate (Bini Darwaja)
  • Explore Zunjar Machi – a narrow ridge known for its sharp drops and scenic views
  • Visit Budhla Machi – a large plateau offering panoramic views of the Sahyadris
  • See the ancient ruins of temples, granaries, and watchtowers
  • Spot old water cisterns still in use today
  • Reach the topmost point for sweeping views of Rajgad, Sinhagad, and Raigad
  • Enjoy the thrill of trekking in mist and clouds during monsoon
  • Witness vibrant wildflowers and lush greenery post-monsoon
