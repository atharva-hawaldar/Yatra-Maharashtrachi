Ellora Caves

The Ellora CavesSprawling across 2 km of basalt cliffs near Aurangabad, the Ellora Caves complex is a breathtaking fusion of art, religion, and architecture, comprising 34 rock-cut caves that were excavated over several centuries. These include 12 Buddhist caves dating from approximately 200 BCE to 600 CE, 17 Hindu caves carved between 500–900 CE, and 5 Jain caves completed around 800–1000 CE. These magnificent structures were patronized by several powerful dynasties including the Chalukyas, Rashtrakutas, and Yadavas, reflecting a rich cultural and religious legacy.
The crowning glory of Ellora is the Kailasa Temple (Cave 16), a monumental achievement of rock-cut engineering and artistry, believed to have been commissioned in the 8th century CE by Rashtrakuta king Krishna I. The temple was carved directly downward from solid basalt rock, with an estimated 150,000 to 200,000 tons of stone removed in the process. Rising nearly 30 meters high and covering a footprint of approximately 50 meters by 33 meters, the temple resembles a freestanding multi-storey Dravidian temple complex complete with intricately carved pillared halls, towering gopurams, spacious courtyards, and grand sculptures. Scenes from the Ramayana and Mahabharata are meticulously etched into the walls, including vivid depictions such as Vishnu’s Narasimha avatar (man-lion form), Ravana shaking Mount Kailash in a show of might, and the sacred Nandi bull patiently watching over Shiva’s abode.
Among the Buddhist caves, Cave 10 (Vishvakarma Cave) stands out with its elegant vaulted chaitya hall, ribbed ceilings that mimic wooden beams, and a serene seated Buddha presiding over the sanctuary. The journey to the caves begins with a scenic 1-hour ferry ride from Mumbai’s Gateway of India, followed by a climb of approximately 120 steps flanked by stalls selling local handicrafts.

Quick Facts

Location:
Aurangabad, Maharashtra.
History:
6th to 10th Century CE, multi-religious complex.
Main Attraction:
Kailasa Temple – world’s largest rock-cut temple.
Unique Design:
Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain caves in one site.

Activities

  • Explore Cave 16 – the magnificent Kailasa Temple, carved from a single rock
  • Visit the Buddhist caves (Caves 1–12) featuring monasteries, meditation halls, and Buddha sculptures
  • Walk through the Hindu caves (Caves 13–29) with intricate carvings of deities and mythological scenes
  • Discover the Jain caves (Caves 30–34) showcasing detailed carvings and serene meditation spaces
  • Take photographs of the pillars, sculptures, and stone-cut facades
  • Spend time at the Archaeological Museum near the entrance
  • Enjoy panoramic views of the Sahyadri hills and surrounding landscapes
