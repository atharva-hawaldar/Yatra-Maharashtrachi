Sahyadri Tiger Reserve

Sahyadri Tiger Reserve is a vast and ecologically vital protected area located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, covering parts of Satara, Sangli, Kolhapur, and Ratnagiri districts. Established in 2007 under India’s Project Tiger initiative, it spans approximately 1,165 square kilometers with about 600 square kilometers of core zone and the remaining area designated as a buffer. The reserve includes two major sanctuaries: Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary and Chandoli National Park, making it one of the largest contiguous forest stretches in the Western Ghats. What sets Sahyadri Tiger Reserve apart is its rugged terrain of high-altitude mountains, deep valleys, rivers, dense forests, and plateaus that create an extraordinary variety of habitats. The forest type is primarily semi-evergreen, moist deciduous, and evergreen patches, interspersed with grasslands and rocky cliffs. The sanctuary’s position in the Sahyadri hill ranges provides essential connectivity for tiger movements between Maharashtra, Goa, and Karnataka. Although the tiger population in Sahyadri is currently low compared to other reserves, it is a crucial tiger corridor and supports a small but significant population of tigers, Indian leopards, sloth bears, wild boars, sambar, barking deer, Indian bison (gaur), and giant squirrels. Camera traps have confirmed the presence of tigers, indicating that the region is gradually re-establishing itself as a secure tiger habitat.
The reserve is equally important for avian and reptilian diversity. It hosts over 230 species of birds, including the Malabar grey hornbill, crested serpent eagle, Indian paradise flycatcher, and various species of woodpeckers and sunbirds. The dense forests and wet areas also provide ideal conditions for amphibians and reptiles, many of which are endemic to the Western Ghats.The Koyna River and the massive Shivsagar Lake play a critical role in maintaining the sanctuary’s ecosystem, offering year-round water sources for wildlife. The region’s high rainfall (due to the monsoon winds hitting the Western Ghats) ensures that the forests remain green and rich in vegetation for most of the year.

Quick Facts

Location:
Western Ghats, covering Satara, Sangli, Kolhapur, and Ratnagiri districts, Maharashtra.
Founded:
Established in 2007 under Project Tiger.
Best Time to Visit:
November to May.
Entry Fees:
Nominal entry fees; separate charges for Vasota boat ride.

Activities

  • Go on a *jungle safari* through Koyna and Chandoli zones to potentially spot Bengal tigers, leopards, gaurs, sloth bears, and diverse ungulates
  • Embark on a *nighttime Nature Experience Programme* (e.g., on Buddha Purnima) from forest watchtowers—ideal for spotting civets, nocturnal birds, and listening to forest sounds after dark
  • Trek or cycle marked forest trails through eco‑zones like Koyna‑Helwak or Bamnoli‑Munavle, including routes to old forts or scenic plateaus
  • Go *early morning birdwatching*, with guides helping locate rare species like Malabar whistling thrush, hornbills, woodpeckers, and many endemic birds
  • Visit *historical forts and waterfalls*, such as Vasota or Pandavgad Fort overlooking the Shivsagar reservoir, or enjoy a refreshing stop at Vajrai Waterfall nearby
