Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary

Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary is a pristine and ecologically significant protected area located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, about 190 kilometers from Pune and roughly 65 kilometers from Mahabaleshwar. Spread over an expansive area of approximately 423 square kilometers, the sanctuary forms an integral part of the Western Ghats, which are globally recognized as a UNESCO World Natural Heritage Site and one of the world’s eight "hottest hotspots" of biological diversity.
The sanctuary is named after the Koyna River, which flows majestically through its landscape, creating the massive Shivsagar Lake — one of the largest water reservoirs in Maharashtra. The terrain is a beautiful mix of thick evergreen forests, semi-evergreen patches, grasslands, valleys, and hills that reach elevations up to 1,100 meters. The region’s high rainfall and rich soil contribute to its lush vegetation and thriving biodiversity.
Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary is a key tiger habitat, though sightings are rare due to the dense forests and the secretive nature of the tigers in this region. Apart from tigers, the sanctuary is home to Indian leopards, sloth bears, sambar deer, barking deer, Indian bison (gaur), wild boars, giant squirrels, and langurs. The forests also support several smaller mammals, reptiles, and amphibians, making it a crucial wildlife corridor that connects other sanctuaries like Chandoli and Radhanagari. The sanctuary is a paradise for birdwatchers and herpetologists, boasting over 250 species of birds including the Malabar pied hornbill, crested serpent eagle, grey jungle fowl, and various species of woodpeckers, kingfishers, and migratory birds that frequent the lake areas. The region is also known for its diverse population of snakes and frogs, many of which are endemic to the Western Ghats.

Quick Facts

Location:
Satara District, Maharashtra, near Koyna Dam and Shivsagar Lake.
Founded:
Declared as a sanctuary in 1985.
Best Time to Visit:
October to May.
Entry Fees:
Nominal entry fee at designated forest check-posts.

Activities

  • Embark on a *jungle jeep safari* through dense forest sections like Vasota and Maharkhor to spot tigers, Indian bison (gaur), leopards, sloth bears, sambar and barking deer
  • Take *guided nature walks or trekking trails*—including the route to Vasota Fort—offering scenic views of the Shivsagar reservoir and dense Western Ghats greenery
  • Go *early morning birdwatching*, with expert guides helping spot rare species like Malabar pied hornbills, Asian fairy bluebirds, heart‑spotted woodpeckers, nightjars and crested goshawks
  • Enjoy *fishing or swimming* in the clean waters of the Koyna River or at safe spots near the reservoir, with expert supervision and riverside picnic lunch
  • Experience a *night safari adventure*, listening to nocturnal calls and spotting civets, deer, bison, leopards by moonlight—and stargazing in the silent forest
