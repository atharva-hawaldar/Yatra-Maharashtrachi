The Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary

The Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary, Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary is a pristine ecological haven located in the Sahyadri Hills of the Western Ghats, about 120 kilometers from Pune and 220 kilometers from Mumbai. Established in 1984, the sanctuary was specifically created to protect the habitat of the endangered species Indian Giant Squirrel (Shekru), which is also the state animal of Maharashtra. Spanning an area of approximately 131 square kilometers, the sanctuary is a crucial part of the Western Ghats, which is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the eight "hottest hotspots" of biological diversity in the world.
The sanctuary is a rich mosaic of evergreen forests, semi-evergreen woods, and grasslands, offering shelter to a wide variety of plant and animal species. It is home to over 275 species of birds, including the Malabar whistling thrush, grey jungle fowl, and several species of hornbills. Apart from the famous Shekru, the forest supports mammals like leopards, wild boars, barking deer, sambar, langurs, and a variety of smaller carnivores and reptiles. The sanctuary is especially popular among birdwatchers, wildlife photographers, trekkers, and nature lovers due to its exceptional biodiversity. The monsoon and post-monsoon seasons (June to February) transform the sanctuary into a lush green paradise, with seasonal waterfalls, dense mist, and a symphony of bird calls creating a magical atmosphere. Treks like the Bhimashankar Trek via Shidi Ghat and Ganesh Ghat are famous routes that pass through thick forests, offering challenging climbs and stunning panoramic views.
At the heart of the sanctuary is the sacred Bhimashankar Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, which draws thousands of pilgrims throughout the year. The combination of religious devotion and ecological richness makes the sanctuary a unique blend of spiritual and natural heritage.
The Bhimashankar region is also the origin of the Bhima River, one of the important rivers flowing through Maharashtra.

Quick Facts

Location:
Covers 131 km² across Ambegaon and Khed talukas in the Sahyadri (Western Ghats), spanning Pune, Thane & Raigad districts. ~110 km from Pune via Manchar; ~213 km from Mumbai.
Founded
Founded In 1984 to protect the Indian Giant Squirrel (state animal).
Best Time to Visit:
Oct–Feb/Mar: Pleasant weather (15–28 °C),

Activities

  • Trek along the *Gupt Bhimashankar trail* through dense forest to a hidden Shivlinga—ideal for spotting Indian giant squirrels, birds, and serene streams
  • Hike up to *Nagphani Point (Duke’s Nose)* or Bombay/Sunset Point for panoramic views of the Western Ghats and surrounding valleys
  • Explore the lush *flora and fauna*, including Indian Giant Squirrel (Shekru), barking deer, leopards, Malabar Whistling Thrush, hornbills, and more
  • Witness the *bioluminescent glow* of Mycena fungi on damp forest floors and tree trunks during monsoon evenings
  • Pay respects at the sacred *Bhimashankar Jyotirlinga Temple*, nestled in the forest, and include nearby spots like Hanuman Lake and tribal groves
