Ganpati Temple

Ganpati TempleAdasa Ganpati Temple is a revered pilgrimage site located in the quiet village of Adasa, approximately 43 kilometers from Nagpur in Maharashtra. Perched atop a small hillock, this temple is known for housing one of the largest and most unique idols of Lord Ganesha in the region. Unlike many other Ganesha temples where the idol is sculpted by artisans, the Adasa Ganpati is a self-manifested (Swayambhu) monolithic idol, standing about 11 to 12 feet tall and carved from a single massive stone. This natural formation adds to the temple’s mystique and spiritual significance.
The temple complex, spread over approximately 10 hectares, is not limited to just the main Ganpati shrine. It also houses around 20 smaller temples dedicated to various deities such as Lord Shiva, Goddess Durga, Lord Hanuman, and other local deities. The primary shrine’s architecture follows the Hemadpanthi style, characterized by its simplicity and durability, with minimal ornamentation and robust stone structures that have withstood the passage of centuries.
Pilgrims reach the temple by climbing approximately 50 to 60 well-maintained steps that lead to the hilltop, offering panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The climb is gentle and accessible to visitors of all ages, making it a popular family pilgrimage spot. Adasa Ganpati Temple holds special spiritual importance as one of the Ashtavinayaks of Vidarbha—a group of eight revered Ganesha temples in the Vidarbha region. The temple is especially vibrant during Ganesh Jayanti and Ganesh Chaturthi festivals when thousands of devotees gather to seek blessings and participate in grand celebrations marked by special pujas, cultural performances, and community feasts. Regular rituals like Sankashti Chaturthi also draw significant local crowds throughout the year. The temple offers a peaceful atmosphere, often described as deeply calming due to its natural surroundings and relatively lesser-known status compared to urban shrines.

Quick Facts

Location:
Adasa village, ~43 km northwest of Nagpur in Savner Taluka.
Deity :
Swayambhu 12-ft monolithic idol of Lord Ganesh — one of Vidarbha’s Ashtavinayaks.
Heritage:
Over a 1,000-year-old hilltop temple, built in Hemadpanthi style; also hosts ~20 smaller shrines.
Access:
Climb ~50 steps or trek a short hill path to the shrine.

Activities

  • Offer prayers to the 11-foot tall monolithic idol of Lord Ganesha, believed to be self-originated (Swayambhu)
  • Climb the 40+ steps to reach the hilltop temple and enjoy the peaceful surroundings
  • Explore the temple complex, which also houses around 20 smaller shrines dedicated to various deities
  • Enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding hills and countryside from the temple premises
  • Attend Ganesh Chaturthi celebrations if visiting during the festival for a vibrant spiritual experience
