Shiva TempleTrimbakeshwar Shiva Temple is one of the most sacred and historically significant temples in India, located in the town of Trimbak, approximately 28 kilometers from Nashik in Maharashtra. Revered as one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the temple holds immense religious importance among devotees of Lord Shiva. The temple is uniquely positioned at the source of the Godavari River, considered one of India’s holiest rivers, which is believed to originate from the sacred Kusavarta Kund situated within the temple premises.
The architectural style of the temple is a classic example of the Hemadpanthi style, constructed from black basalt stone. The temple is adorned with intricate stone carvings, beautifully sculpted pillars, and a gracefully designed dome. The main sanctum houses a distinctive Jyotirlinga, which is not a standalone Shiva Linga but a small, naturally formed depression housing three faces representing the Hindu Trinity — Brahma, Vishnu, and Mahesh (Shiva) — a rare representation not commonly found in other Jyotirlingas across India. This tri-faced Jyotirlinga is constantly adorned with a jeweled crown, which is displayed to devotees only on Mondays.
Trimbakeshwar is also renowned as a center for Vedic rituals and sacred Hindu ceremonies, particularly for performing the Narayan Nagbali, Tripindi Shraddha, and Kalsarpa Shanti rites, which are believed to cleanse ancestral and karmic doshas. These rituals attract thousands of pilgrims from all over India who come to seek spiritual liberation and ancestral peace.
The temple complex is surrounded by scenic hills and is part of the majestic Brahmagiri mountain range, from where the Godavari River begins its sacred journey. Pilgrims often trek up Brahmagiri Hill to witness the river’s origin and to visit other nearby temples and caves. The environment around the temple is serene and lush, particularly during the monsoon season, enhancing its spiritual ambiance.

Quick Facts

Location:
Trimbak, ~28 km from Nashik, one of the 12 Jyotirlingas in India
Deity & Significance:
Sanctuary of Lord Shiva—an origin point of the Godavari River from the sacred Kusavarta Kund.
Architecture:
Built in Hemadpanthi black stone, featuring a gold-plated pinnacle—constructed by Peshwas.
Rituals:
Pilgrims perform rituals like Nagbali, Tripindi Tarpan, and Kumbh Snan in the kund.

Activities

  • Offer prayers at the sacred Jyotirlinga, one of the twelve revered Shiva shrines in India
  • Explore the temple’s stunning Hemadpanthi architecture built with black stone
  • Take part in traditional rituals like Narayan Nagbali and Kalsarpa Shanti performed by local priests
  • Visit the origin point of the Godavari River, located near the temple
  • Walk around the temple complex and admire the beautifully carved stone sculptures and shrines
