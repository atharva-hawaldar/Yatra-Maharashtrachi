ISKCON PUNE Temple

ISKCON TempleISKCON NVCC (New Vedic Cultural Center) Temple in Pune is one of the largest and most vibrant ISKCON temples in Maharashtra, dedicated to the teachings of Lord Krishna and the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition. Located in the serene hills of Kondhwa, this spiritual and cultural center offers a peaceful escape from the busy city life and stands as a beacon for those seeking devotion, learning, and community service.
Spread across six acres of beautifully landscaped grounds, the ISKCON NVCC Temple complex features two main shrines built in distinct architectural styles—one in traditional North Indian design and the other in South Indian Dravidian style. The twin temples are adorned with intricately carved stonework, majestic gopurams (temple towers), marble flooring, and ornate archways that offer a blend of ancient artistry with modern construction. The primary deities worshipped here are Sri Sri Radha Vrindavanchandra, along with Lord Venkateswara, Lord Jagannath, Lord Baladev, and Subhadra Devi in adjoining sanctums. The temples are alive with daily rituals including Mangala Aarti, Bhoga offering, Shringar Aarti, and Gaura Aarti, each accompanied by traditional bhajans and the rhythmic chanting of the Hare Krishna maha-mantra, creating a deeply spiritual atmosphere that resonates throughout the complex.
Apart from being a place of worship, ISKCON NVCC is also a thriving educational and cultural hub. It regularly conducts Bhagavad Gita study sessions, youth leadership programs, spiritual discourses, workshops on meditation, and cultural activities for children and adults alike. The temple community actively promotes values such as compassion, simplicity, and service through seva (volunteer work) and food distribution programs like the Akshaya Patra midday meal scheme for underprivileged children.

Quick Facts

Location:
Kondhwa, Pune—largest ISKCON center in the city.
Deities:
Radha Krishna and Venkateswara idols in twin sanctums.
Architecture:
Built in 2013 on 6 acres with North & South Indian style temples, domes, classrooms, and gardens.
Activities:
Daily aartis, bhajans, and scriptural classes on Bhagavad Gita & Srimad Bhagavatam.

Activities

  • Participate in the *daily aarti rituals* — Mangala Aarti, Sringar Aarti & Guru Puja, Raj Bhoga Aarti, and evening Sandhya Aarti — at their scheduled times for devotional atmosphere
  • Attend *guided meditation sessions*, spiritual discourses on Bhagavad‑Gita and Srimad Bhagavatam, or workshops and youth programs held throughout the week
  • Explore the *temple architecture and art* — admire marble pillars with Gopi and elephant carvings, gold‑plated altars, mural ceilings, and separate Radha‑Krishna and Balaji temples in North and South Indian styles
  • Visit the *Vrindadevi temple and flower farm*, see its Tulsi cultivation, serene pond setting, and serene green lawns maintained sustainably with solar panels and cow shelter nearby
  • Join *festival celebrations* like Janmashtami, Radhashtami, Holi, Ratha Yatra — enjoy kirtans, cultural programs, special retreats (e.g., "Amrutotsava") and family spiritual events
