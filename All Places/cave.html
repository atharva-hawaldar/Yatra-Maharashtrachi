CAVES

Karla Caves

Karla Caves are among Maharashtra's oldest rock-cut Buddhist sites, dating back to 2nd century BCE. Known for their intricate carvings, grand chaitya hall, and wooden beams, these caves were centers of meditation for monks. They remain a popular destination for history and architecture enthusiasts.

Bedse Caves

Bedse Caves, located in Pune district, date back to the 1st century BCE during the Satavahana period. These caves feature Viharas, Chaityas, and inscriptions in Brahmi script. Their serene environment makes them an ideal trekking and heritage site for tourists seeking peace and history.

Ellora Caves

Ellora Caves near Aurangabad consist of 34 rock-cut temples representing Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain traditions. The Kailasa temple is the highlight, carved out of a single rock. These caves stand as an architectural marvel of ancient India and are listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Pandavleni Caves

Pandavleni Caves in Nashik are a group of 24 rock-cut caves dating back to 1st century BCE. They were originally used as viharas and meditation halls for Buddhist monks. The caves feature inscriptions, sculptures, and water tanks, reflecting the rich Buddhist heritage of India.

Pitalkhora Caves

Pitalkhora Caves, located in the Satmala ranges, are among the oldest rock-cut caves in Maharashtra. Dating to the 2nd century BCE, they showcase exquisite carvings and stupas. The caves were major Buddhist centers during the Hinayana phase of Buddhism in India.
