Tarkarli Beach

Located sin Malvan taluka, Sindhudurg district — approximately 546 km from Mumbai and 410 km from Pune — Tarkarli is often called the “Queen Beach of Konkan” for its long stretch of pristine white sands and crystal-clear turquoise waters at the confluence of the Karli River and the Arabian Sea .
This scenic coastal village is flanked by suru trees and backed by rolling hills, offering idyllic sunrise and sunset views travelkonkan.in. It’s one of the few places in India where you can enjoy SSI-certified scuba diving and snorkeling in shallow, safe waters — including vibrant coral reefs near Sindhudurg Fort and the submerged “Tsunami Island” — under professional instructors using international-standard equipment Alongside this, you’ll find parasailing, banana-boat rides, jet-skiing, kayaking, and dolphin-spotting boat trips, making Tarkarli a full-fledged water-sports hub Getting there: Accessible by road via NH66 (Mumbai–Goa Highway); daily buses operate from Mumbai, Pune, and Goa. The nearest railheads are Kudal and Sindhudurg (~30–35 km away), with the upcoming Chipi/Sindhudurg airport (16 km) expected to boost connectivity . Stay & culinary highlights: A wide range of accommodations—from MTDC resort and houseboats to homestays and private beachside lodges—offer you direct beach access . Don’t miss the local Malvani seafood thalis—fish curry, prawns, pomfrets—alongside the region’s kokum sharbat and fresh Alphonso mangoes Home-style eateries and beach stalls provide a genuine taste of Konkan cuisine
