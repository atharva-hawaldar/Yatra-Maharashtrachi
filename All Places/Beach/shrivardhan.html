Shrivardhan beach

,Shrivardhan BeachLocated in Raigad district, approximately 180 km from Pune, Shrivardhan Beach is a serene, gently curving black-sand shoreline that extends over a kilometer, framed by casuarina trees and historic temples. This coastal town is steeped in Peshwa-era heritage, with the centuries-old Shrivardhan Ganesh Temple and surrounding shrines offering a spiritual dimension to your beach visit.
The calm waters and smooth sands make it ideal for family outings, peaceful walks, and light swimming. Reaching Shrivardhan is convenient via coastal roads or well-connected state buses and private vehicles from Pune and Mumbai.
Accommodation includes charming heritage guesthouses, modest beach retreats, and coastal homestays—all serving Konkani delicacies; don’t miss the fresh pomfret fry, bangda (mackerel), and homemade sol kadhi. Beyond the beach, visitors can rent boats for estuary tours, cycle through nearby fishing villages, and explore local creeks dotted with watertolerant trees. Best visited from November to February, when the weather is pleasant and evenings are cool, though the monsoon months (June to September) turn the surrounding Sahyadri foothills a lush green, with soothing rains. Evening beach walks with a light sweater are highly recommended. Guests should carry comfortable walking shoes, a power bank, and modest swimwear, and be aware that electricity supply in smaller stays may be unpredictable.

Activities

  • Take long, peaceful walks on the clean sandy shore surrounded by coconut and mango groves
  • Try water sports like boating, jet-skiing, parasailing, surfing, banana boat rides, or buggy rides
  • Go for a dolphin-spotting boat trip with local fishermen in the calm Arabian Sea
  • Visit the bustling Khalcha Jivana port to watch the evening fish auction and interact with fishermen
  • Enjoy local Konkan seafood specialties and refreshing drinks like sol kadhi and kokum sharbat
