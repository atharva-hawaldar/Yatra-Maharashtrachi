, Perched majestically atop a rugged Sahyadri peak near Pachad, Raigad Fort stands at 1,356 m above sea level and served as the Maratha Empire’s proud capital after Shivaji Maharaj’s coronation in 1674.
Visitors can reach the summit by climbing approximately 1,737 stone steps over a 2–3‑hour trek or opt for a scenic 4‑minute cable car ride on the Raigad Ropeway, which ascends 420 m.
The fort’s remaining ruins include the Queen’s Quarters, the royal Durbar, Jagadishwar Temple, stepping stones into Maratha grandeur. A poignant highlight is Shivaji’s samadhi, accompanied by the memorial to his loyal dog Waghya, while the abrupt drop at Takmak Tok reflects the fort’s grim past. Lush waterfalls and verdant vistas bloom during monsoon, whereas winter months offer crisp air and panoramic clarity. Essentials like potable water, eateries, and lodging are available near the ropeway base and MTDC cottages, but visitors should pack snacks and water for the trek. With clear directions from Pune and Mumbai via NH4/NH17 or rail to Veer, Raigad is both accessible and enriching—a must on any Maharashtrian heritage trail.

Activities

  • Take a peaceful walk along the golden sandy beach and enjoy the calming sea breeze
  • Try exciting water sports like jet skiing, parasailing, and banana rides
  • Visit the historic Suvarna Ganesh temple near the beach
  • Explore local coconut plantations and experience the village charm
  • Relish fresh seafood delicacies at small beachside eateries
